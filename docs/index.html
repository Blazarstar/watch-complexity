Machine learning framework to analyze complex networks

Artificial Intelligence from experimental research on computational social science

Find Out More

WatchComplexity is a Machine Learning framework to understand and analyze complex networks and more in general complex data.

It is a collection of clustering techniques inspired by social science and communication theories.

Algorithms

Ranked Influence Typology

Detect the type of influence that each node holds within a network.

The algorithm detects not only the influence played by every node inside a network, but also how it contributes to the overall network (word-of-mouth, content creation, and diffusion of information are just examples).

Given a large dataset of connections as input, it provides a ranking of all nodes by influence score, reporting their typology of influence. Every node plays a certain role in the network and affects the other nodes in its own different way.

Technically, it is a clustering algorithm useful for understanding very large and complex networks that are also impossible to visualize, grouping their components according to their internal flows.

Network Similarity

Measures the similarity between two or more networks.

The comparison between networks is conceived not only in terms of similarity of their nodes, but also from a functional point of view, watching their structures and the flow of information by a semantic perspective.

Given some distributions of influences measured using Ranked influence typology algorithm, and the number of connections, it provides two different misures of similarity: a quantitative one, along with another more qualitative.

Finding how similar are personal profiles of users, similarities between maps of interactions or how strong are the similitudes in patterns of data, are only examples.
